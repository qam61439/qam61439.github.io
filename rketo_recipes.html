Chopped Pork BBQ
Simple, classic and your dad, granddad or uncle may already have this in place!

Ingredients:

  • 1 Boston Butt
  • Salt
  • Optional: Pepper
  • Optional: Sugar-free store bought or homemade BBQ rub.
Directions:
Season meat and bake (or smoke) for 1.5 hours per pound (until temperature reaches 195 to 205 depending on preference). Serve with your favorite sugar-free BBQ sauce.
Optionally, you may wrap it in butcher paper or aluminum foil once the temperature reaches 160-170 to decrease cooking time and decrease the risk of oversmoking.

Mayonnaise
Once you try homemade mayo, you will likely never want store bought mayonnaise again!
Ingredients:

  • 1 Cup Extra Light Olive Oil
  • 1 Egg
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Dijon Mustard (or other mustard types if you'd like to experiment)
  • 1 Tablespoon Vinegar or Lemon Juice (Note: White vinegar is the norm, but other vinegars can be used.)
  • 1/4 Teaspoon Keto-Friendly Sweetener
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Salt
  • Optional: 1 to 3 Tablespoons of water. This makes the mayonnaise "fluffy".

Directions:
Using an immersion blender, crack the egg into a cup or jar (which the blender will fit into). Then add remaining ingredients except for oil on top of the egg. Lastly, pour the oil into the jar. Place the immersion blender into the jar and pulse until the mixture begins to emulsify (look creamy). Then keep pulsing and work the blender upward until the contents look like mayo. After this is complete, add a lid onto it and place into the refrigerator.
Note: This recipe can also be made with a standard blender or a food processor. You will have to experiment to get the proper texture. (Typically, the smaller the bottom of the mixing device, the better.)

Cole Slaw
Using the homemade mayo from above, this cole slaw recipe will be a hit!
Ingredients:

  • 1/2 Head Cabbage, shredded (or diced)
  • 1/2 to 3/4 Cup Mayonnaise [start with 1/2 cup and work your way up depending on texture]
  • 1/2 Cup Keto-Friendly Sugar Substitute
  • 1/4 Cup Lemon Juice
  • 1/4 Teaspoon Keto-Friendly Sweetener
  • 1 Tablespoon sugar-free sweet relish
  • Optional: 1/16 Teaspoon Xanthan Gum (This keeps the cole slaw from running).

Directions:
Combine all ingredents in a bowl and mix together. If using Xanthan Gum, ensure it is mixed quickly into the mixture to prevent clumping.

Belgian Waffle
This may work as a regular waffle and possibly a pancake recipe

Ingredients:

  • 48 Grams Almond Flour
  • 1 Egg
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Cinnamon
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Baking Powder (prefably aluminum-free)
  • 1/8 Teaspoon Salt
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Vanilla Extract (the higher the quality the better)
  • 1/4 Cup Heavy Cream
  • 1 Tablespoon Keto-Friendly Sugar Substitute
Directions:
Mix dry ingredients together separately. Mix wet ingredients together separately. Then combine and pour into hot waffle iron for 3 to 5 minutes. Serve.

Meal Ideas

BBQ
Continue to make BBQ the same way you always have. Use sugar-free sauce if using store bought sauce. If making homemade sauce, use keto-friendly sugar substitutes or sugar-free sweeteners instead of traditional sweet ingredients.
Some regional BBQ sauces such as Eastern NC sauce are already keto-friendly in their original form.

Greens
Whether it be turnip greens (i.e. turnip salad for some), mustard greens or collard greens, these are usually keto friendly. For those that add a bit of a sweetener to their greens will need to find a sugar substitute. However, traditional family recipes can otherwise be maintained.

Breakfast
Eggs and bacon (or whatever meat you prefer) are fine. There are low carb recipes for oatmeal, grits, pancakes and waffles. Additionally, vegetables can be substituted in for side items. An example of this is using grated cauliflower to make a healthier hash brown.

Stews
Substitute out potatoes with lower carb root vegetables such as turnips, radish or rutabaga. The classic beef stew recipe with beef chunks, potatoes, onions and optionally carrots can still be done by swapping out the potatoes for one fo the aforementioned lower carb root vegetables.

Fish
Frying fish in a cast iron skillet is a southern tradition. While wheat flour nor traditional seed oils are keto, fried fish can achieved using unflavored protein powder, almond flour and either avocado oil or light olive oil. Experiment with seasoning and build a unique recipe!

Homemade Sugar-Free Ice Cream
Using a low-glycemic sugar substitutes, the same tradition of making homemade ice cream can still be done. While there will need to be some experimentation to revamp your family's recipe, this can be done. The benefit in that by making the recipe keto, the ice cream will be extremely creamy and flavorful for all to enjoy!

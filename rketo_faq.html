Frequently Asked Questions

What are some examples of what I can eat on Keto?
Take waffles for instance. If you love waffles, you can still eat them, but substitute out regular flour with almond flour, which is lower in carbs and use a low glycemic sugar substitute. Also, use a syrup that is sugar-free (that is also low-glycemic). If you love bacon and eggs, you can still eat bacon and eggs. Just make sure the bacon is not cured with sugar. Additionally, you can have fruit such as strawberries orphans blueberries. Keep in mind, the goal is low carbs.

I can eat bacon and eggs and actually improve my health? Get outta here!
Absolutely. Here's one even better. You can still eat all the BBQ you like. You can eat whatever meats you like. Just make sure the amount of carbs you consume with it are as low as possible. It helps to have low-carb vegetable dishes along with meals whenever possible to get the best health benefits out of your meal. Additionally, season your food as you've always done in the past. Minus the sugar!

I've done some reading and I've heard of intermittent fasting. What is this?
Ever heard of food that "sticks to the ribs"? The fat is what does that. It satiates your hunger and prevents the ongoing need to eat, which is usually common with high carbohydrate foods. Addionally, there are studies that mention health benefits provided by intermittent fasting. You will not feel as if you are starving yourself and the "hangry" episodes will become a thing of the past.

What is "Keto Flu"? I don't want to get sick!!!
In a nutshell, when you make adjustments to your diet, your body has to make adjustments on how to function and what to draw energy from. Keto flu is what you feel when your body transitions to running on fat as opposed to carbohydrates. Typically, taking electrolytes during this period (sugar free, of course) is a good way to make it through this time period.

What about cheat days? I still want to eat certain foods with my family.
You can still have your cheat days, but make it infrequent. Especially during the early timeframe. However, there needs to be a clear distinction between cheat days and normal days. Cheat days should be infrequent to achieve the maximum benefits of keto.

Avocados??? Yuck!!!
Avocados are very healthy and have health benefits for keto, but you don't have to eat avocados to be successful with keto. Smile!

I see you mention meat quite frequently, but I am vegan/vegetarian. Is keto for me?
Absolutely! There are many ways to achieve the benefits of a ketogentic diet. There will be a bit more effort involved, but this can be easily achieved.

Ok, I get it. Cut out sweets and starches. Anything else I should cut out?
There are some cooking oils made from seeds (such as vegetable oil or canola oil) are usually not a healthy source of fat. There are other oils that can be used in place of these that provide better health benefits.

This is weird. Eat fatty foods and improve my health?
Much like anything else in life, taking on a keto diet is a personal decision. However, many have succeded and it is a decision that both you and your doctor will have to agree on. We are here simply to provide an alternative to the norm to improve health where many have had past issues.

Ok, this sounds interesting. Anything else you think I should know?
Do some research and keep it as simple as possible. While there are certain foods that you may have deep attachments to, look at the big picture. Also, there are cookbooks that can help. Your meals do not have to be fancy at all. Just keep it simple, keep the carbs down and make sure your food is as natural as possible. There is an old saying that if you can't grow it or kill it, you shouldn't eat it.

How To Get Started

  • Take inventory of current foods you have at home.
  • Remove foods that are high sugar/high carbohydrate.
  • Create a list of low carb meals you will likely enjoy.
  • Grocery shop and add to your food inventory.
  • Do not be afraid to experiment with new foods and recipes.
  • Do not let setbacks discourage you from moving forward.

What is Keto?

The Ketogenic Diet, more commonly known as Keto is a high-fat, low carbohydrate diet. There are many benefits to the keto diet including weight loss, improvements with blood sugar/insulin levels, better control of appetite, stress relief, mood improvements, reduced inflammation and improved blood pressure.

As with any diet, there will be a need for improved food choices and exercise. The purpose of this site is to bring awareness of new options in food choices, which many are quite familiar and may benefit health on long-term basis. As always, is recommended that you consult with a medical doctor prior to beginning any diet/exercise regimen.

Keto Fundamentals

Keto consists of low carb, high fat and moderate protein meals. With this being the case, to be successful with keto, the following will need to be removed from daily meals:

  • Sugar: Sugar contains a high amount of carbohydrates and needs to be removed for the purpose of regulating blood sugar/insulin response.
  • Flour: Flour is a foundational component for a lot of meals such as biscuits, pancakes, waffles, fried foods, etc. There are viable substitutes to flour that can be used to be successful with keto.
  • Starches: Starchy vegetables such as corn and potatoes will need to be removed from the diet as well to have results. There are other vegetables that are delicious and much lower in carbohydrates.
  • Some Fruits: Berries are typically fine in that they are low in carbohydrates; however other fruits are usually high in carbohydrates and do not align with the low carbohydrate requirement of the keto diet.

These are general guidelines to take into consideration when considering a keto diet.
